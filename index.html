Iguazu Falls Panoramic Overflight

Overflights over Iguazu Falls offer a breathtaking perspective of this natural wonder, which straddles the border between Argentina and Brazil. Here’s what you need to know:

aERIAL ROUTES

Scenic flying tours are the most popular way to experience Iguazu Falls from above.
Our bird take off from the Cataratas del Iguazú International Airport (IGR).
During the flight, you’ll soar over the Iguazu River, capturing stunning views of the falls, rainforest, and the Devil’s Throat—the largest waterfall in the system.

Experience the Majesty

Iguazu Falls, also known as Cataratas del Iguazú (in Spanish) or Cataratas do Iguaçu (in Portuguese), consists of 275 individual waterfalls.
The falls are nestled within the Iguazu National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Overflights provide a bird’s-eye view of the falls, revealing their immense scale, lush surroundings, and the mesmerizing interplay of water and jungle.

booking tips

Reserve in advance to secure your spot, especially during peak tourist seasons.
Prepare for an unforgettable adventure as you witness the cascades from a whole new perspective!

Duration and Impact

We offer two options. The complete tour of 1 hour, taking flight over Iguazu Falls, Triple Frontier point, la amistad bridge and Itaipu Dam,
Our 30 minutes tour is a panoramic overflight over Iguazu falls and returning to Cataratas Del Iguazu International Airport.
It’s a once-in-a-lifetime experience, allowing you to appreciate the falls’ grandeur and the raw power of nature.

Hotel Rincon del Socorro

In 2001, Douglas Tompkins, a philanthropist and conservationist, acquired the farm Rincón del Socorro to create a 74,131- acre natural reserve. Tompkins and his team restored and expanded the old buildings of the farm, removed the cattle from the fields, eliminated hundreds of miles of fences and initiated the biggest process of reintroduction of locally extinct fauna (REWILDING) in the Americas. Giant anteaters, pampas deer and peccaries have already been released. Part of this process concluded with the donation of lands for the creation of the Iberá National Park which, together with the provincial reserve, constitutes the great Park Iberá.
Rincón del Socorro is immersed in The Iberá National Park where diverse natural ecosystems can be found: several temporarily flooded grasslands known as malezales, beautiful Caranday palm groves, broomsedges, savannas, espinal woodlands and gallery.

Mocona Waterfalls

The Mocona Falls, also known as Saltos del Mocona, are a spectacular natural wonder located in the northeastern province of Misiones, Argentina. Although not as famous as the Iguazú Falls, they are equally worth visiting. Here’s what you need to know:
What are Saltos del Mocona?These falls stretch for 3 kilometers along the Uruguay River.
Unlike most waterfalls that run perpendicular to the river, Mocona Falls run parallel to it, which makes them truly unique.
The falls vary in height from 5 to 10 meters.

Location and Access: Mocona Falls are situated in Moconá Provincial Park.
The park is in the Litoral Argentino region, near the town of El Soberbio.
Nearest airports are Posadas (PSS) and Puerto Iguazú (IGR), both approximately 300 kilometers away.
If you arrive at either airport, doing an overflight is recommended due to limited transportation options in the area.

Posadas

Posadas is a vibrant city located on the left bank of the Paraná River, which separates it from the neighboring city of Encarnación in Paraguay. As the most populous city in Misiones, Posadas serves as the region’s administrative, commercial, and cultural hub. Its modern waterfront, known as the Costanera, invites visitors to enjoy unique experiences such as riverside dining, water activities, city tours, and lively nightlife with live bands, bars, and craft breweries.

Ibera Wetlands

Overflights and destination hotel Rincon del Socorro at Ibera Wetlands, you will be inmersed into one of the greatest forest in the world

Rewilding Initiative

Three decades ago, Iberá was a degraded backwater due to commercial foresting, illegal hunting, and cattle ranching. However, thanks to a transformational rewilding initiative led by entrepreneurs Doug and Kristine Tompkins, the wetlands have been restored. The couple purchased large tracts of land, not just to protect them, but to restore entire ecosystems. As a result, Iberá is now one of South America’s premier wildlife-watching destinations.

one of the biggest wetlands around the world

Located in north-east Argentina, the Iberá Wetlands cover a vast 13,000 square kilometers. This pristine wilderness consists of lakes, floodplains, grasslands, and subtropical forests, all nestled within the Corrientes Province, approximately 640 kilometers north of Buenos Aires.
